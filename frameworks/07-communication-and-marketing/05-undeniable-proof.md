# Undeniable Proof — Close the Imagination Gap

> *"That's what undeniable proof is — it's removing the imagination gap. The gap between what someone has to picture and what they can actually see. The smaller the gap, the harder it is to say no."*

## The principle

People say *no* to what they can't picture. Asking someone *to believe* something is possible — you make it hard for them.

When you **show** them it's possible — belief is no longer required.

## How to look at it

Skip abstract promises. Work with:
- Concrete demonstrations
- Visible results
- Specific examples
- Reproducible patterns

## The two gap types

### Before the sale (Sales)
Imagination gap = distance between *"what you say"* and *"what the buyer must imagine"*.

→ Close via: demos, case studies, live examples, trial access, sample output.

### After the sale (Operations / Soft Shop)
Imagination gap = distance between *"what you claim the business can do"* and *"what buyers / investors / partners see"*.

→ Close via: reports, data, demonstrations of execution capability.

## Worked example

A director wanted an Amazon show. Instead of saying *"I can do this"*, she shot a short film — explicitly framed and shot so any executive would see *"Yes, she can direct suspense drama like Ozark."*

→ Imagination gap eliminated. No belief needed.

## How to apply it

**On every sales conversation:**

| Claim | Imagination gap | Proof |
|---|---|---|
| "We deliver in 2 weeks" | large | Case study with timestamps |
| "We bring conversion lift" | large | Before/after data + metric |
| "We understand your industry" | medium | Specific questions / insights in the first call |
| "We're reliable" | large | Visible track record |

**On Soft Shop / stakeholder relationships:**

> *"You said if I did these five things, it would be $75M. We did the things. Here are the numbers."*

→ Promised, delivered, documented. Trust earned.

## The "Show, don't tell" rule

Before any sales or pitch claim, ask:
1. *Can I demonstrate this instead of asserting?*
2. *Do I have a concrete example instead of a generality?*
3. *Can the recipient experience it themselves instead of imagining?*

If all three "yes" — proceed. If none "yes" — refine.

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Identify top 3 claims in your current sales materials.
- [ ] Per claim: is there an imagination gap?
- [ ] Per gap: develop concrete proof (case, demo, example).
- [ ] Update materials.
- [ ] Follow-up communication integrated with proof.

## What proof can be

- Mini-audits / mini-reports free before sale
- Recordings of live calls / consultations (with permission)
- Concrete numbers with context
- Step-by-step walkthroughs
- Personal experience reports from existing customers
- Mathematical models / transparency of assumptions

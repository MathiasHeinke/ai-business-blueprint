# Memo Culture — WAFAM (Write A F***ing Memo)

> *"No memo, no decision. No memo, no meeting. Fear has no place on paper."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

At acquisition.com, every decision requires a memo. Every meeting needs a pre-shared memo. No memo — no discussion.

The memo forces **clarity** and **eliminates theater** in meetings.

## Why this works

| With memo | Without memo |
|---|---|
| Clarity of thought | Mushy discussion |
| Forced preparation | Time wasted catching up |
| Discussion on substance | Discussion to understand |
| Institutional knowledge | Context lost |
| Onboarding via memo reading | Onboarding takes weeks |
| Asynchronous input | Loudest wins |

## How to look at it

> *"Fear has no place on paper. You see it and you're like — oh, that is what I was thinking. I'm not afraid about that anymore."* — Sam Shvatsa

Memos break vague worries into specific questions others can answer. They make thinking visible.

## The WAFAM rules

### Rule 1: Every decision is a memo
If someone says *"Should we do X?"*, the answer is: *"WAFAM."* — Write it down.

### Rule 2: Every meeting has a pre-read memo
No memo, no meeting. Period. You save 80% of meeting time.

### Rule 3: Memo is read before the meeting
"Read this memo with shared understanding." In the meeting we only address the questions in the memo.

### Rule 4: Onboarding = memo reading
New person? Read the last 20 memos. They're at team level.

### Rule 5: SOP effect
Similar project? Dupe a memo, adapt, rewrite — the decisions are already made.

## Why this matters for solo operators too

> *"It's more valuable for solo operators going Mach 3 with their hair on fire than even large business owners. You see it on paper, fear has no place on paper."*

For solo: memo = a structured talk with yourself. Yields clarity where the daily chaos dissolves it.

## How to apply it

**Implementation in 3 steps:**

1. **Today:** Write your next pending decision as a memo (template in `../09-templates-and-worksheets/01-memo-template.md`).
2. **This week:** Before every meeting, ask for / supply a WAFAM.
3. **This month:** Set up a memo archive system (Notion / Google Drive / repo) — searchable by topic and date.

## Where to store memos

Recommended structure:
```
/Memos/
├── 2026/
│   ├── 04-April/
│   │   ├── 2026-04-15-pricing-update.md
│   │   ├── 2026-04-22-hire-content-lead.md
│   │   └── ...
```

→ chronological + quarter-navigable.

## AI acceleration

> *"Today it's so much easier — voice chat AI, here's everything I want to do, my disorganized thoughts, put this in the memo framework."*

Workflow:
1. Speak / dump raw thoughts into AI
2. AI prompt: *"Structure this in our memo framework: Story so far, Issue, Recommendation, Open Questions"*
3. Iterate to sharpen
4. Share

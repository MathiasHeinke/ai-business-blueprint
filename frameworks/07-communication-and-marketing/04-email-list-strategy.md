# Email List Strategy — One-Third of Net Worth

> *"I will tell you, probably one-third of my net worth has been created from my email list. We built a billion-dollar business off my email list."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

In a world of scroll feeds and algorithms, email is the **only medium where every message is evaluated individually**. You control directly when and how your message reaches the recipient.

## Why email is so powerful

| Property | Email | Social feed |
|---|---|---|
| Per-message evaluation | individual | scroll mode |
| Algorithm in between | no | yes |
| Personal context | personal | mass feed |
| Opt-in (raised hand) | active | passive consumption |
| Platform risk | low (standard) | high (reach can change) |
| Conversion | high | low |

> *"You don't sign DocuSign over text message. Email is considered to be a personal, serious platform."*

## How to look at it

An email list is an **ownable asset** — no one can take it away. If Instagram disappears tomorrow, the account is gone. If Google Mail disappears tomorrow — the list is exportable and transferable.

> *"If I lost my email list, it would be like losing a third child."* — Sam Shvatsa

## How to build email lists

### 1. The Lead Magnet
A clearly defined incentive against which people give their email.

Bad lead magnets:
- "Subscribe to newsletter"
- "Get updates"

Good lead magnets:
- Concrete tool / template
- Result-promising mini-training
- Personally relevant information on demand
- Free trial access with value

### 2. Capture mechanism
Where do you capture? Multiple touchpoints:
- Main page (popup, inline, footer)
- Content pages (in posts, at the end)
- Social bios → lead magnet → email
- Conferences / events → physical capture
- Webinars

### 3. Nurture sequence
The first 5–10 emails after signup are critical. They establish value and expectation.

### 4. Regular communication
Weekly or biweekly. Consistency beats frequency.

## What to send

| Mix | Share |
|---|---|
| Value (insights, lessons, behind-the-scenes) | 70% |
| Soft sell (connections to offerings) | 20% |
| Direct sell (promo, launch, limited) | 10% |

Pure marketing emails burn the list. Pure value emails don't monetize. The mix wins.

## How to apply it

**Setup phase (30 days):**

1. **Define lead magnet** (aligned with target audience)
2. **Pick tool** (ConvertKit, ActiveCampaign, MailerLite, Klaviyo, ...)
3. **Build capture on main page + content pages**
4. **Welcome sequence** (5–7 emails over 14 days)
5. **Define weekly cadence**

**Growth phase (ongoing):**

- Every content touchpoint leads to the list
- Conversion optimization
- List hygiene (remove inactives — deliverability)
- A/B test subject lines

## KPIs

| Metric | Benchmark | Critical |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 25–40% | <15% |
| Click rate | 3–8% | <1% |
| Unsubscribe rate | <0.5% | >2% |
| List growth | +5–10% / month | stagnation |

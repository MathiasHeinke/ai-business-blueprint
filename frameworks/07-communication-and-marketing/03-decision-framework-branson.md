# Decision Framework — The Branson Lesson

> *"Most people don't have a framework for making decisions. Therefore, they make dumb decisions."* — Richard Branson

## The principle

Good decision makers don't differ from bad ones in intelligence or information — they differ in **method**.

Distilled framework:

## The four steps

### 1. Understand the Context
Question: *Where is the shared information? What do we all know?*

→ Only when everyone is on the same factual ground does the decision begin.

### 2. Isolate the Issue
Question: *What is the actual problem we're solving?*

→ Often the named problem isn't the real one. Isolation reveals it.

### 3. Accept the Risk
Question: *If we make this decision — what breaks? What can we live with?*

→ Risk isn't *to avoid*, it's *to accept*. Conscious acceptance reduces fear.

### 4. Map the Decision
Question: *What are the next steps? Who does what when?*

→ A decision without implementation isn't a decision.

## How to look at it

Before any major decision: 4-step exercise. Takes 15–30 min and replaces 80% of gut-feeling rumination.

## How to apply it

**Decision template (included in every memo):**

```markdown

- What we know (3–5 points)

```

## Worked example: Telos

- **Context:** Beverly Hills business, $300M volume, CEO stealing.
- **Issue:** Not *"CEO out"*. Real issue: *"Business has value, but no operator with integrity."*
- **Risk:** Reverse mortgage on family home → if business breaks, personal ruin.
- **Map:** Take over → 5-year plan → soft shop → real sale.

→ 10x in 5 years. But: deliberate risk acceptance, not luck.

## Anti-pattern: "Gut feeling"

Gut feeling is *pattern recognition* from lived experience. If you haven't lived the pattern, there's no gut feeling. Most entrepreneurial decisions are new — you have no pattern. → Framework replaces gut feeling, until pattern emerges.

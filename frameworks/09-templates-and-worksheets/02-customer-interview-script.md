# Customer Interview Script — The Two Magic Questions

> Duration: 20 min. Purpose: discover the real value prop, not confirm what you think.

---

## Preparation (5 min before the call)

- [ ] Notes doc open, labeled with customer name + date
- [ ] Recording tool ready (with permission)
- [ ] Mindset reset: *I'm here to listen, not to explain.*

---

## Opening (2 min)

> *"Thanks for taking the time. I'm taking time right now to make sure we focus our energy on the right things. I have two simple questions — and I promise I won't explain or justify during your answers. I'll just listen. Sound good?"*

→ Get permission, mention recording.

---

## Question 1 — Take-away

> *"Imagine I took something we currently do for you away next week — and our collaboration would no longer be valuable to you. What would that one thing be?"*

### If they list multiple:
> *"If you had to pick only one — which would it be?"*

### If they answer generically ("everything is important"):
> *"Let's get specific. Remember a moment in the last 4 weeks when you were most grateful we exist. What was happening?"*

### Follow-ups:
- *"Can you tell me more about that?"*
- *"What does that mean for you day-to-day?"*
- *"When did you last consciously notice this?"*
- *"How much value (numbers / time) is this for you?"*

→ **Don't explain, don't confirm, don't justify. Only ask.**

---

## Question 2 — Add-on

> *"Now reverse: if I added one thing to our collaboration tomorrow that you don't have today — and that would make you stay forever — what would that one thing be?"*

### If vague ("more updates"):
> *"Specifically: what would change in your day-to-day or your results if it existed?"*

### If they say there's nothing ("everything's good"):
> *"Even if everything's good — if you had a magic wand, what would you improve or add?"*

### Follow-ups:
- *"How often do you think about this gap?"*
- *"Do other providers do this?"*
- *"What would it mean if you had it?"*

---

## Bonus question (optional, if time)

> *"What was the deciding factor for you choosing us — and not the alternative?"*

→ Useful for sales material.

---

## Closing (1 min)

> *"Thank you for your honesty. What you said genuinely helps. I'll pull patterns from multiple calls and may come back with an update. If anything else occurs to you — a short email is enough."*

---

## After the call (5 min — today, not later!)

- [ ] Capture answers *verbatim*, don't paraphrase
- [ ] Note your own reaction: what was surprising?
- [ ] Transfer to tracking sheet (below)

---

## Pattern analysis (after 10 calls)

1. Which take-away answers recur? (count exact or semantically similar answers)
2. Which word or concept appears in >50% of answers?
3. Which add-on wishes show a pattern?
4. How did *former* customers answer differently than *current* customers?

→ The top pattern becomes the **new value-prop hypothesis**.

---

## The most important rule

**Don't convince. Don't explain. Don't justify.**

When you feel the urge to explain — bite your tongue, ask *"Tell me more."*

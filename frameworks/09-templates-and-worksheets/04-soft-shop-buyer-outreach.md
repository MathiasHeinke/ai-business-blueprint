# Soft-Shop Buyer Outreach — Templates for Market Validation

> Purpose: contact 3–5 plausible buyers for a non-binding initial conversation, to obtain the "five things" list.

---

## Who is a plausible buyer?

Three categories:

1. **Strategic buyer** — larger player in the same industry, can leverage synergies
2. **Financial buyer** — PE fund, family office, investing in the industry
3. **Complementary buyer** — company in adjacent industries that would use you as a bolt-on

→ Aim for all three categories in your list, not just one.

---

## Outreach email — template

```
Subject: Strategic exploration — [Our company] / [Their company]

Dear [Name],

I run [Our company]. We are [short description, 1 sentence] currently
doing [size indicator — revenue, team, customers, whatever fits].

Over the coming months we are evaluating strategic options for our
next growth phase. Given your position in [market / area], you are
one of a small number of parties whose perspective would be most
valuable to us.

This is not a negotiation — it is an honest exploration. How would
you see us from your side? What gaps would you want closed before
a closer relationship or combination would make sense?

Would you have appetite for a 30-minute initial conversation over
the next 2–3 weeks? I'd value the opportunity to learn from each
other.

Best,
[Name]
```

---

## Pre-call package (sent before the call or shown during)

### One-pager

- What we do (1-sentence value prop)
- For whom (target audience)
- How (model, channels, differentiation)
- Growth (3-year revenue, margins, trend)
- Team (size, key roles)
- What we're exploring (open, no commitment)

### Optional, depending on trust level

- 3-year P&L (revenue, EBITDA / profit)
- Customer concentration (top 5, % of revenue)
- Growth story: where do the next 3 years come from
- Cap table (rough)

→ NDA if more detailed numbers are shared.

---

## In the call — the magic question

After intro & discussion (~20 min into the 30 min):

> *"Based on what you've seen and heard — how would you value us? A rough indication, not a binding offer."*

Answer: *"About $X."*

> *"And why not 1.5X?"*

→ **Here's the gold.** Answer: *"You'd need to do these five things."*

Take notes. Ask follow-ups. Understand.

---

## The negative-space question

Equally valuable:

> *"As you look at our business, are there areas you assign **little or no** value to? That would be important for us to understand."*

→ What buyers see as worthless is a candidate for **cut** (`../01-core-principles/04-growth-by-subtraction.md`).

---

## After the call — documentation

Within 24h, into your memo system (see `01-memo-template.md`):

```markdown
# Soft-Shop Memo — [Buyer name] — [Date]

## Valuation indication
$X — $Y

## The "five things" list to reach [higher valuation]
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...
4. ...
5. ...

## Areas without value (from buyer's view)
- ...
- ...

## Additional insights / market signals
- ...

## Action items for us
- ...
```

---

## Follow-up after 12 months

> *"We spoke a year ago. You said: if we did [the five things] we'd be at [valuation]. We did them. Here are the numbers. Would this be a good moment for an update conversation?"*

→ Trust building. Preparation for a real sale, if it ever becomes relevant.

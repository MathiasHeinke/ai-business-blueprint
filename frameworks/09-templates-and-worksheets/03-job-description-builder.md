# Job Description Builder — Pain → AI → A-Player JD

> Goal: in 60 min, create a JD that attracts only A-players.

---

## Step 1 — Write pain / aspiration raw (15 min)

Don't filter. Don't make it "professional." Write it as it feels.

### If it's pain-of-today:

```markdown
# Raw pain — [Date]

What's currently going wrong that I need this person for?

- [Pain point 1] — how often? how loud?
- [Pain point 2]
- [Pain point 3]

What does it cost us?
- Time:
- Quality:
- Money:
- Sleep:
- Customers:

How does it feel to live with daily?
[Free-write — emotional is fine]

What would change if this person were here?
- Day 1:
- Month 1:
- Quarter 1:
```

### If it's growth-of-tomorrow:

```markdown
# Raw aspiration — [Date]

What if we had this person?
- What new would we do?
- What area would we open?
- What possibilities emerge?

What would a typical day look like for this person?
[Free-write]

What would this mean in 12 months?
[Free-write — think big]
```

---

## Step 2 — AI translation (10 min)

**Prompt:**

> *"Here is my raw, unfiltered text about [pain / aspiration]: [content from Step 1]*
>
> *Translate this into a job description that attracts A-players. Follow this structure:*
>
> *1. Opening hook: why should this person come here?*
> *2. The context: who we are, in which phase*
> *3. Your impact: 3 outcomes (not tasks!)*
> *4. The problem you solve: specific and honest*
> *5. Your everyday: what you do in a typical week, with whom*
> *6. You likely fit if: 3 concrete skills/qualities*
> *7. You likely don't fit if: 2 anti-patterns*
> *8. What we offer: compensation range, equity/phantom, growth path, culture (specific)*
> *9. How to apply: specific self-selection question*
>
> *Be specific, avoid buzzwords, address this person directly so they recognize themselves."*

---

## Step 3 — Refinement (20 min)

Review the output:

- [ ] Sounds like **us** or generic?
- [ ] Are outcomes concrete (not "manage day-to-day")?
- [ ] Compensation range visible (not "competitive")?
- [ ] Real self-selection question?
- [ ] "Don't fit if..." section there and honest?
- [ ] Reading the JD, would I think "that could be me/someone"?

→ If "no" anywhere: rebuild that section.

---

## Step 4 — Build in self-selection question (10 min)

Examples of good self-selection questions:

> *"In your application, please include a concrete example where you systematized a founder-dependent operation."*

> *"In your application, write a paragraph about how you'd diagnose a conversion funnel that needs to go from 30% to 35%."*

> *"Send us a 60-second Loom video explaining what you loved most about your last job and what you liked least."*

→ Filters out C/B-players without effort.

---

## Step 5 — Active recruiting (ongoing)

JD published is *not* enough. Recruit actively:

- [ ] LinkedIn: 10 fitting profiles identified + personally contacted
- [ ] Network referrals: 5 people from 10-10 list asked: "Know anyone?"
- [ ] Communities the target person is active in
- [ ] Conference speakers / content creators in the niche

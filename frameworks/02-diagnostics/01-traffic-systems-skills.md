# Traffic / Systems / Skills

> *"In a service business, it is three things. Traffic, systems, and skills."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Every service business has exactly three components. A problem in one slows the others. Diagnose in this order:

### 1. Traffic
**Job:** Fill the funnel with opportunities.
**Diagnostic question:** Do you have a reliable, near-unlimited source of qualified leads?

### 2. Systems
**Job:** Convert opportunities into appointments / cash / contracts.
**Diagnostic question:** Are leads automatically progressed to bookings, contracts, or deals — without you manually pulling each one?

### 3. Skills
**Job:** Deliver and get paid.
**Diagnostic question:** Who fulfills? Is the skill transferable, or does everything depend on the founder?

## How to look at it

If you're not growing, there's exactly one reason. One of the three is **the bottleneck**. Scaling means: identify the bottleneck, fix it, identify the new bottleneck, fix it — repeat.

It's never all of them at once. It's always one.

## How to apply it

**Quarterly TSS audit:**

| Area | Status (1–10) | Bottleneck? | Next action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traffic | __ | yes/no | __ |
| Systems | __ | yes/no | __ |
| Skills | __ | yes/no | __ |

**Rule:** Only address the area that is *currently* the bottleneck. Improvements elsewhere are wasted because the system clogs at the bottleneck anyway.

## Common case: skills bottleneck

Typical for small agencies and consultants: traffic ok, systems ok, but **the founder is the delivery**. Until that skill is transferred, there's no scaling.

→ Concrete: build SOPs, train a junior, hire your first delivery person, run a co-delivery transition phase, then phase yourself out.

## When all three are broken

If traffic, systems, and skills are all underperforming: **systems first**. (See `04-plumbing-before-water.md`.) Otherwise:
1. The first leads have a bad experience → reputation damage.
2. You burn money on traffic that doesn't convert.

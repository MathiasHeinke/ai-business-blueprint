# Bottleneck Finder — The "Assume Success" Method

> *"The easiest way to figure out the thing to focus on is to pick the thing in your head and then assume that you've accomplished that goal. Does everything work?"* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Instead of asking *"What should I improve?"*, ask *"If I'd solved this one thing — would the rest keep up?"*

If the answer is *no* — you've found the real bottleneck.

## The method

**Step 1:** Identify a presumed primary problem.
*Example: "We need more leads."*

**Step 2:** Assume the problem is solved. Dramatically solved.
*"We now have 10x the leads we have today."*

**Step 3:** Walk it through:
- Can we convert them?
- Can we deliver?
- Do we have the people?
- Do we have the systems?

**Step 4:** Wherever it breaks — *that's* the actual bottleneck.

## How to look at it

Most founders solve the *current* problem and stand confused at the next one, because they never asked *"What comes after success?"* The bottleneck finder reverses that.

## Worked example

**Assumption:** *"We double revenue next month."*

Walk it:
- Doubling customers / projects → can our delivery capacity hold?
- Who onboards? Is that role doubleable?
- Cashflow — can we front the work?
- Tools and systems — do they hold the load?
- Communication with existing customers — does it slip?

Every "no" is a bottleneck that must be solved **before** the lead doubling, not after.

## The Mr Beast example

Mr Beast is face-driven. Yet he's not on set every day. He shows up for a few hours, films multiple episodes in one shoot, then the system continues.

He recognized the bottleneck (his own face), **deliberately** built structures to compress his presence, instead of letting the system consume him.

# One Value Prop Pitch

> *"That grew the business from $300M to $3.4B, 10× in 5 years with that one value prop pitch. Nothing else. That's all it was."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

A **single, crystal-clear value proposition**, in one sentence, is more powerful than ten differentiated selling points. It becomes the filter for every decision in the company.

## The Telos story compressed

1. Reverse mortgage, business taken over, zero real-estate experience.
2. 30 agents asked: *"What would you not want to give up?"*
3. Unanimous answer: *"Being here saves me a day a week."*
4. Resulting value prop: **"Come to Telos because we give you back at least one day a week. What would you do with another 52 days a year?"**
5. Every decision after that filtered: *Does this help agents save a day?* Yes → do. No → don't.
6. 10x in 5 years.

## Anatomy of a strong value prop pitch

| Element | Telos example | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Concrete unit** | "One day a week" | measurable |
| **Real feeling** | "52 days a year" | scales emotionally |
| **Reframe** | What would you do with that? | triggers imagination |
| **Filter usability** | "Does this help?" yes/no | every decision filterable |

## How to look at it

A value prop is not a marketing slogan. It's the **operational filter** for the entire company.

> *"We need a $10 million ad budget. Does this help our agents save them one day a week? No, we're not going to do it."* — Sam Shvatsa

Decisions on initiatives, hires, cuts, ad spend — all run against the value prop. If it doesn't filter, it's too vague.

## How to apply it

**Step 1:** Ask the two magic questions to 10 customers (see `01-the-two-magic-questions.md`).

**Step 2:** Find patterns. Which answer recurred? What was the *one thing* behind the take-away question?

**Step 3:** Compose into a single sentence with:
- A concrete, measurable outcome
- An emotionally scaling form
- A reframe question

**Step 4:** Test: can you filter every current initiative through it? If not — sharpen.

**Step 5:** Internalize — every team member knows the sentence by heart.

## Anti-pattern: the feature pitch

> *"We offer editing, producing, social, graphic design, web, and VFX in one place."*

That's not a value prop. That's a **feature list**. It filters nothing. It inspires nothing. It's internally convenient and externally forgettable.

# Talking to Customers — The Conversations We Avoid Most

> *"We fear talking to our clients and our customers. We really fear that. We think — because we're smart — that they want all of these things. They don't care about most of those things."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Founders avoid customer conversations because:
1. We fear negative feedback.
2. We believe we already know what customers want.
3. We assume more delivery is always better.

All three are usually wrong.

## What customers actually don't want

> *"The extra calls. The one-time touches. The Loom videos. The written reports. We think they do because it sounds good to us, but they don't care."*

**You build complexity because it sounds good *to you*** — not because customers need it. Telos agents wanted *one day a week*. Nothing else. No bonus trainings, no Loom videos.

## How to look at it

Every assumption about your customer is a hypothesis until they confirm it. Guesses cost time, energy, and false differentiation.

## The fear, dismantled

| Fear | Reality |
|---|---|
| "They'll say something negative." | Mostly they're flattered to be asked. |
| "I'm wasting their time." | 20 min for mutual benefit — not waste. |
| "They might churn if I ask." | Those who would churn will anyway. Asking earlier often prevents it. |
| "I'll hear things I don't want to change." | Those are exactly what you need. |

## How to apply it

**Customer conversation cadence:**

| Frequency | Format | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly | 5 existing customers, 20-min call | Pulse check, Q1+Q2 |
| Quarterly | 10 customers including former | Pattern analysis |
| Every offboarding | Exit interview | What was actually missing? |

## The most common mistake

In the call, **explaining** instead of **listening**. The moment a customer answers, the urge is to justify, explain, solve. Wrong.

**Rule:** in the call, your job is to tolerate silence and ask follow-ups. Every explanation contaminates the answer.

## Opening script

> *"I'm taking time right now to make sure we focus our energy on the right things. Could you spare 20 minutes to answer two questions for me? It's not about telling me what you think I want to hear — it's about the honest answer."*

## Concrete actions

- [ ] This week: request 3 customer calls.
- [ ] Practice the script (or read it — that's fine).
- [ ] Conduct calls, write **verbatim** notes.

## Permission to be uncomfortable

Some customers will say things that sting. That's not the end — that's the information you'd have paid a consultant for.

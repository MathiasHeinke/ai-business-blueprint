# Scalability — The Don Shula Lesson

> *"Every single play that they mapped out ended up in a touchdown. No other coach in the history of the NFL plans that way."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Don Shula, legendary Miami Dolphins coach, didn't plan for *small yard gains*. Every single play was structurally designed so that it *could* become a touchdown.

Translated: choose activities whose best-case is a *large* outcome — not ones whose best-case is a small lift.

## How to look at it

For every initiative, ask:
- **Yard activity:** best case 5–10% improvement
- **Touchdown activity:** best case 10x outcome possible

Touchdown activities often take the same or less effort. Seek them deliberately.

## The standard filter

> *"What would it take for this to be $10 million? What would it take for this to be $100 million? When you put that frame on it, you start to realize the current model doesn't work because complexity fans out."*

For every activity:
1. What's the $10M version of this?
2. What's the $100M version?
3. If the current version doesn't fit that path — is it really strategic?

## How to apply it

**Scoring matrix for new initiatives:**

| Initiative | Effort (1–10) | Best-case impact | Touchdown potential? |
|---|---|---|---|
| __ | __ | __ | yes/no |

Touchdown potential = yes if:
- Scales without linear effort growth
- Reproducible
- Self-reinforcing (network effect, reputation, recurring)

## Anti-pattern

> *"Run spider-three-wide-banana and you'll get eight yards."*

That's the standard mindset: solid plays, small gains. Works for survival, not for scaling.

## Worked example

**Yard play:** A 5-min tweak to your sales script → 2% conversion lift.

**Touchdown play:** A new distribution via JV with a complementary provider → potentially 10x audience.

Both may take similar effort. Only one is a touchdown setup.

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Run your top 5 current initiatives through the touchdown filter.
- [ ] Add at least 1 pure touchdown initiative per quarter.
- [ ] Don't eliminate yard plays — but consciously size them.
- [ ] In strategy sessions: ask the $10M / $100M question by default.

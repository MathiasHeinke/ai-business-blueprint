# Exit Mindset — Build As If You'd Sell Tomorrow

> *"The goal is not the exit. The goal is options. When you don't know your options, you don't have any."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Build your business *as if* you were selling tomorrow — not because you *want* to sell, but because that standard forces:
- Systems that work without you
- Clear processes and documentation
- Reproducible value creation
- Transferable skills inside the team

A business that doesn't run without you is a job — not an asset.

## Why this is a hack

**Successful ≠ Sellable.**
- A successful business may depend on you daily.
- A sellable business runs without you.
- The sellable version is also the *better* one to own.

## How to look at it

For every structural decision, don't only ask *"Does this work?"* — ask *"Would this give an outside buyer confidence?"*

Buyers want to see:
- Documented processes
- A diversified customer base (no concentration risk)
- More than one person carrying critical skills
- Reliable, growing revenue
- SOPs for onboarding, delivery, sales

## How to apply it

**Sellability audit (once a year):**

| Criterion | Status (1–10) | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Documented processes | __ | __ |
| No concentration risk (>20% any single customer) | __ | __ |
| Delivery skill on more than one person | __ | __ |
| Recurring revenue / predictability | __ | __ |
| Brand IP, domains, contracts clean | __ | __ |
| Financials (P&L, bookkeeping) clean | __ | __ |

Lowest 2 = next quarter's priority.

## The Apple lesson

> *"Steve Jobs the second time he came back built something so systematized, so clearly defined in its values and operations that it didn't just survive without him. It's still thriving."*

The best companies aren't built around a person. They're built around a **system**.

## The double-effect

1. If you never sell — you have a better business to own.
2. If you do sell — you're ready.
3. Optionality: you can decide *because* you have the option.

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Run the sellability audit.
- [ ] Make the two biggest gaps quarterly initiatives.
- [ ] Set up a Soft Shop (see `02-soft-shop-strategy.md`).
- [ ] On every hire or tool decision: *"Does this make a future sale easier?"*
- [ ] Schedule a documentation sprint — SOPs for the top-3 processes.

## What doesn't have to happen

You don't have to sell just because you prepared for it. The plan is optionality, not exit.

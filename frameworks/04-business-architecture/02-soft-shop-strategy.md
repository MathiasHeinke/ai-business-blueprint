# Soft Shop Strategy — Annual Market Validation Without Actually Selling

> *"Every summer I used to take the business to market as I was actually planning on selling it. I identified three to five key buyers, put a package together, went through that process."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

You **simulate** a sale once a year with real potential buyers. You learn:
- What your business is actually worth
- Which five things would lift the value 50%
- Which areas have **zero value** to a buyer (= deprioritization candidates)
- Whether you're a competent, adaptive operator (from the buyer's view)

## How to look at it

A soft shop is the most valuable **free strategic consulting** in the world. Buyers have a financial interest in being honest. They won't tell you what you want to hear — they tell you what they see.

## The process

### Step 1 — Identify buyers
- 3–5 plausible buyers (competitors, larger players, PE funds, strategic acquirers)
- Not too small, not unrealistic — realistically interested

### Step 2 — Build the package
- One-pager: what you do, for whom, how
- 3-year P&L (revenue, margins, trends)
- Customer concentration (concentration risk)
- Growth story: where does the next 3 years come from?
- Team setup, key roles
- IP / brand / differentiation

### Step 3 — Outreach (template in `../09-templates-and-worksheets/04-soft-shop-buyer-outreach.md`)

> *"We're evaluating strategic options for our business. Would you have appetite for an initial exploratory conversation?"*

Clear: non-binding, NDA if needed.

### Step 4 — The magic question

After the first conversation:
> *"Based on what you've seen — how would you value us?"*

Answer: *"$X."*

> *"And why not 1.5X?"*

Answer: *"To get there, you'd need to do these five things."*

→ **Those five things are your business plan for the next year.**

### Step 5 — Repeat, prove

Next year, same buyer:
> *"You said if we did these five things we'd be worth $1.5X. We did them. Here are the numbers."*

→ Builds trust, raises valuation at the *real* sale, if it ever comes.

## What else you learn

Buyers also tell you what has **zero value**:
> *"Why didn't you assign any value to the Orange County market?"*
> *"We don't see synergies there and it's losing money."*

→ *"Excellent. I'll deprioritize that part of the business."*

## How to apply it

**Annual soft-shop sprint (1 week):**

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Mon | List 5 plausible buyers |
| Tue | Assemble the package |
| Wed–Thu | Outreach |
| Fri | Set first meetings |
| Following | Take meetings, ask the magic question, document |

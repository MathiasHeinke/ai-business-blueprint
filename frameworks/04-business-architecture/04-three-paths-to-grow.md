# Three Paths to Grow — Self / Outside / Employees

> *"Do you grow the business yourself, do you grow the business with someone's help, or do you grow the business with your employees?"* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

There are three structural ways to grow a business — and most founders only know one. Optionality means *knowing* all three, even if you only choose one.

## The three paths

### 1. Self — Self-fund
You put cash in, you keep all of it.
- **Pro:** 100% ownership, full control
- **Con:** Your capital is locked, your risk
- **When:** Clear profitability, secured cashflow, slower scale acceptable

### 2. Outside — External equity
Bank, PE, Angel, VC.
- **Pro:** Leverage, faster scaling, network
- **Con:** Co-owners with expectations, reporting, possibly exit pressure
- **When:** Capital-intensive scale, market window, proven model

### 3. Employees — Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP)
You sell part of the business to your employees.

**Worked example** (wealth management firm):
- 50% sold to employees
- Employees only had to contribute 10% of that themselves
- Bank financed the other 40%
- Founder received $50M, then $250M from PE
- **Bonus:** PE wanted the deal *because* the 50 employee-shareholders were locked in → no defection risk

- **Pro:** A-player retention, founder liquidity, more attractive to PE
- **Con:** Complex to set up, doesn't fit every model
- **When:** Service businesses (consulting, accounting, wealth mgmt) where person-loyalty matters

## How to look at it

You don't think *"bootstrap or raise"* — that's binary and limiting. You think: *"Which of the three paths fits our current phase and goals?"*

## Phantom equity as a special case

If you want to bind someone without giving real shares → **phantom equity**. See `../05-people-and-talent/03-phantom-equity.md`.

# Simplicity as Strategy

> *"To do great things, we must do fewer things."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Complexity feels productive. Most of the time, it's avoidance. The largest businesses in the world were built on a **single dominant core** before they diversified.

- Skims → just shapewear
- Good American → just jeans
- Instagram → just square photos
- Drybar → just blowouts
- Mailchimp → first 5,000 contacts free

## Two critical advantages of simplicity

### 1. Scalable systems are easier to build
A simple model can be diagnosed. With ten variables you never know what's broken. With three you do.

### 2. Marketing and sales become trivial
**76% of customers are more likely to recommend brands that deliver simple experiences.** Brands leading on simplicity outperform stock market averages by over 200% (Siegel+Gale).

If your customer can't explain what you do in one sentence, they won't recommend you.

## How to look at it

Every service line, every feature, every initiative is **attention debt**. Default: no. Adding requires justification. Removing does not.

## How to apply it

**The reduction test (once a quarter):**
1. What's the simplest version of your business that still carries all your strategic goals?
2. Which *one* thing, if removed, would not destroy the business?
3. If you could only work on one thing in the morning — which would it be?

**The explanation test:** Can a customer summarize your core service in one sentence to a friend? If not, simplify.

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Write your current value proposition in one sentence.
- [ ] Run it past three current customers: "Would you describe us this way?"
- [ ] Audit every active initiative against the sentence.
- [ ] Anything that doesn't support it is a candidate for cut.

## Real example

Telos Properties: from $300M to $3.4B with a single value prop:

> *"You save at least one day a week."*

Every decision after that was filtered: *Does this help our agents save a day per week?* Yes → do it. No → don't. Even on a $10M ad budget question.

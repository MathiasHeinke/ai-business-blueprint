# Recruiting Superstars — Pain → AI → Job Description

> *"Whenever you hire somebody, you're looking for one of two things: solving the pain of today or the growth of tomorrow."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Generic job descriptions attract generic applicants. A-players respond to JDs that read **as if written for them** — because they name your concrete pain or their concrete aspiration exactly.

## The workflow

### Step 1 — What is the gap?
Two possibilities:
- **Pain of today** — you're drowning, you need relief
- **Growth of tomorrow** — you want to open a new area

### Step 2 — Write the pain or aspiration **raw**
Unfiltered. As raw as it feels.

**Pain example:**
> *"I have no time to respond to customer emails on time. Quality slips. Customers complain. I'm sleeping poorly because of unanswered threads. I'm losing deals because follow-ups fall through."*

**Aspiration example:**
> *"Wouldn't it be incredible if we had someone who orchestrates our entire content pipeline — from idea to production to distribution — without me being involved daily?"*

### Step 3 — Feed it to AI
> *"Translate this into a job description that attracts A-players. Be specific. Avoid jargon. Write so that the person reading it feels addressed."*

### Step 4 — Refine the JD
- Keep real pain / aspiration visible
- Real responsibilities, not generic roles
- What this person will move (outcome, not tasks)
- Context, team, tools

## Why this works

> *"It's not that they are an A player. It is that they are an A player for you. That's the fit."*

A-player for you ≠ A-player for someone else. You're not looking for the *best*, you're looking for the *most fitting*.

The A-player reads the JD and thinks: *"I'm exactly what they need."*

## How to look at it

JDs are not HR paperwork. They are your **strongest pre-selection tool**. 30 minutes invested in a real JD saves 30 hours of interviews with the wrong people.

## How to apply it

**Hiring workflow:**

1. Write pain / aspiration raw (15 min).
2. AI generation (10 min).
3. Refine in the team (30 min).
4. Publish JD + actively recruit (see `02-a-player-job-descriptions.md`).
5. Outreach to fitting profiles — don't wait for them to find you.

## Active recruiting

> *"Stephen Bartlett spends the majority of his weeks interviewing and recruiting more than anything else."*

A-players are currently employed. They don't apply. You go to them.

| Method | When |
|---|---|
| LinkedIn outreach | Specific role, clear ideal profile |
| Network referrals | A-players know A-players |
| Communities / conferences | Pre-emptive pipelining |
| Headhunters | Senior / specialized |

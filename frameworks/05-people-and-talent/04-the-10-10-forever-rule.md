# The 10-10 Forever Rule — Wealth Is a Who-Strategy

> *"Who are the 10 people that you would invest in for the next 10 years who would pay you forever?"* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Wealth is generated less by *what* (investment, strategy, asset) than by *who* (which relationships compound over long time horizons).

If you select 10 exceptional people and consistently invest in them — time, attention, referrals, opportunities — over 10 years, the returns compound in ways no investment product can match.

## How to look at it

> *"Instead of investing in the next hot stock or hot startup, you say: I'm going to put [Name] in my 10-10 forever. Whatever they say, I'm going to do. Whatever I can do to support them, I'm going to do — because I know that this relationship is going to be worth more than anything else."*

This isn't networking. It's **relationship investing** with compounding returns.

## The two filters

A person belongs in your 10-10 if:

1. **You'd invest in them** — you believe in their trajectory over the next 10 years.
2. **They'd pay you back** — not financially, but in mutual value exchange over the next 10 years.

If they fail either filter → nice acquaintance, not 10-10.

## Investment can be

- Time (calls, meetings, joint projects)
- Referrals (warm intros, advocacy)
- Knowledge (mentoring, sparring)
- Money (investment, small bridges)
- Access (to your network, spaces, tools)
- Attention (real, not surface-level)

## The anti-pattern

Collecting LinkedIn connections. Mass-trading business cards at conferences. Treating "nurturing your network" as a quarterly task.

→ That's breadth. 10-10 is depth.

## Where this leads

In 5 years, your 10-10 list is the most valuable asset class you own. It opens doors money can't, closes deals contracts can't, and gives you clarity consultants can't.

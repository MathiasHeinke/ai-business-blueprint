# A-Player Job Descriptions — Why the Generic JD Fails

> *"The A player actually reads your job description. They actually care. So if you think your generic job description doesn't matter, you are wrong."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

A-players read JDs *slowly and carefully*. B- and C-players skim. A JD full of buzzwords filters out exactly the candidate you want — because A-players don't recognize anything they personally relate to.

## Anatomy of an A-player JD

| Element | Generic | A-player |
|---|---|---|
| **Context** | "We are a fast-growing company" | "We do $X revenue, growing Y/month, Z customers — phase: P" |
| **Role** | "Operations Manager" | "You orchestrate so our delivery pipeline runs without founder intervention" |
| **Outcome** | "Responsible for day-to-day" | "In 90 days we deliver to 30% more customers without quality loss" |
| **Pain** | (missing) | "Current pain: founder bottleneck. You remove it." |
| **Growth** | "Career opportunities" | "You'll be the first ops hire — building the team beneath you" |

## The self-selection question

In the JD, include one specific question or detail that only an A-player can understand or answer.

**Example:**
> *"In your application, please include a concrete example where you systematized a founder-dependent operation. Without this detail, we don't continue reading."*

→ filters immediately. B-players overlook / ignore. A-players deliver.

## How to look at it

JDs aren't *"permission to apply"* documents. They're your **funnel top**. Bad JD = bad funnel top = weeks wasted on the wrong calls.

## Structure template (copy)

```
[Opening hook — what brings this person to you]

THE CONTEXT
- Who we are, in 2 sentences
- Phase, size, model
- Why we're hiring now

YOUR IMPACT
- Outcome 1
- Outcome 2
- Outcome 3
(Outcomes, not tasks!)

THE PROBLEM YOU SOLVE
- Specific. Raw. Real.

YOUR EVERYDAY
- What you do in a typical week
- Who you work with

YOU LIKELY FIT IF
- Concrete skill / background 1
- Concrete skill / background 2
- Concrete personality / way of working

YOU LIKELY DON'T FIT IF
- Anti-pattern 1
- Anti-pattern 2

WHAT WE OFFER
- Compensation (range, not "competitive")
- Equity / Phantom Equity
- Growth path
- Culture (specific, not "we're like family")

HOW TO APPLY
- Specific filter / self-selection question
- What we read / what we ignore
```

## Why "you don't fit if..." matters

A-players read the "doesn't fit" list. If they don't see themselves there, they feel confirmed → higher application quality. B-players often see it as a warning and don't apply → you save time.

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Replace existing JDs with the template structure.
- [ ] Switch to outcome language, shorten task lists.
- [ ] Build in a self-selection question.
- [ ] Always show compensation range.
- [ ] Add a "doesn't fit if..." section.

# Asking for Help — The Underrated Acceleration Skill

> *"To solve any problem, three questions: What could I do? What could I read? Who could I ask?"* — Jim Rohn

> *"I sent her an email. 20 minutes later, she replied: Is this a joke?"* — Sam Shvatsa, on his $10K coach offer

## The principle

Asking for help accelerates more than any other skill. Yet it's the most avoided — because it feels like showing weakness.

## Why we avoid it

| Inner voice | Reality |
|---|---|
| "I should figure this out myself" | Others have already solved this — hours instead of months |
| "I'm wasting their time" | The right people value being asked |
| "I'm showing weakness" | Asking shows initiative + coachability |
| "I'm bothering them" | A precise ask is a gift, not a burden |

## The two modes: Ask vs Recruit

### Ask
- A moment, a question, a specific help.
- No ongoing relationship.
- Mostly free, if the question is precise.

### Recruit
- An A-player who walks with you over time.
- An investment.
- Comes with an **offer**.

→ Both have their place. Both are underused.

## The $10K coach move

What made it work:
- Low stakes for the coach: no calls expected, no fixed meetings
- Specific: only prioritize email replies
- Symbolic investment ($10K, all he had): seriousness signal
- Not "Can we grab coffee?" — a specific request with clarity of expectation

> *"I would just hope you would prioritize responding to my emails."*

→ Low bar, high clarity, real investment. She said yes.

## How to apply it

### On every problem: three questions
1. *What could I do?* (own action)
2. *What could I read?* (books, posts, courses)
3. *Whom could I ask?* (specific person)

→ Question 3 gets skipped 80% of the time. That's the biggest acceleration.

### When you ask, be specific

**Bad:** *"Got any tips on marketing?"*
**Better:** *"We have a 4% conversion funnel that's stalled for 3 months. You wrote you took a similar funnel from 4% to 7%. Could I have 15 minutes for a focused brainstorm?"*

### For recruiting: the email template

```
I saw you speak today / followed your work. [Specific point that
made an impression.]

I'd like to offer you [symbolic amount] as a sign of my seriousness
if you can be my coach for [period]. I expect no scheduled calls,
no meetings — I won't bother you. I'd just hope you'd prioritize
responding to my emails.

[Name]
```

Low bar, high clarity, real investment.

## How to look at it

Asking for help isn't a weakness symptom — it's **efficiency**. Solving alone is romantic, but expensive.

Coachability is a skill. Those who can ask, learn faster.

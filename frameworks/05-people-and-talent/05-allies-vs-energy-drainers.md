# Allies vs Energy Drainers — Who Belongs in Your Orbit

> *"Cut out the wrong ones. The people who make you feel like you need to keep your dream to yourself. The people who make you feel guilty for working on your dream. The people who listen to what you want to build and make you feel like it's too big — instead of making you realize it might actually be too small for what you're capable of."*

## The principle

Pursuing big goals is hard. With the wrong people in your orbit, it's impossible. With the right ones, it becomes manageable — sometimes even light.

Allies aren't cheerleaders. Allies are people who believe in you *when you don't believe in yourself*.

## The two lists

### Allies (List 1)

| Quality | Example |
|---|---|
| Believe in your trajectory | "You'll figure this out" — even in dark weeks |
| Give honest feedback | tell the truth, not the flattering version |
| See you bigger than you see yourself | remind you of your potential |
| Invest without quid-pro-quo | help because you matter — not for return |

### Energy Drainers (List 2)

| Quality | Example |
|---|---|
| Dim your light | "Aren't you too busy?" |
| Skepticism as identity | see problems where solutions exist |
| Guilt-tripping | "You don't have time for me anymore" — when you work |
| Comparison energy | distrust when you grow |

## How to look at it

You can't cut all energy drainers from your life — some are family, old friendships, colleagues. But you can **consciously dim** how much bandwidth they get.

Allies get bandwidth. Energy drainers get the minimum civility.

## How to apply it

**Inventory (alone, 30 min):**

Top 15 people you spend regular time with:
- Who builds you up?
- Who drains you?
- Who's neutral but consumes attention?

**Concrete steps:**

1. **Reinforce allies:** more time, more depth, more investment (see `04-the-10-10-forever-rule.md`).
2. **Maintain neutrals:** civil, but no energy investment.
3. **Reduce drainers:** drop bandwidth. Not abruptly — but consciously less.

## The most important relationship

If your life partner doesn't believe in you — that's not "also a problem", that's *the* problem. The allies inventory starts at home.

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Run the inventory honestly.
- [ ] Identify 3 allies who are under-invested — give them deliberate time this month.
- [ ] Identify 3 energy drainers whose bandwidth must go down.
- [ ] Quarterly revisit: update the list.

## The difficulty

This isn't a 1-month project. It's a permanent question: *"To whom am I giving my attention right now, and why?"*

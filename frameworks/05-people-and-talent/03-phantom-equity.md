# Phantom Equity — Ownership Feeling Without Ownership Complications

> *"Phantom equity. You bring an A-player on board. They feel like an owner. If we sell, they get a piece. By the way, if you leave, this goes away."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

Phantom equity is a **contractual promise** that mirrors real shares — without the legal, tax, and corporate-governance complications of actual ownership.

## What it is

A separate document stating:
> *"If we sell the company, person X receives a defined percentage of the sale proceeds (e.g. 10%). If the person leaves, the claim disappears."*

No shares, no voting rights, no shareholder status, no change to operating agreements.

## Why it's powerful

| Real equity | Phantom equity |
|---|---|
| Cap table change | Separate doc |
| Voting rights | None |
| Tax complication | Earnings-pay (income tax at trigger) |
| Permanent (even on exit) | Lapses on departure |
| Active shareholder | None |

## When to use it

- An A-player you want to think like an owner
- A liquidity event is the shared goal
- You want retention *without* ownership risk
- The person isn't (yet) at co-founder level

## How to apply it

**Implementation steps:**

1. **Founder team decision:** Who gets phantom equity, at what level?
2. **Plan document drafted** (with legal counsel — country-specific; in DE pay attention to tax implications)
3. **Discuss together with the person** — explain that this is phantom, not cap table
4. **Vesting clause** — e.g. 4 years, 1-year cliff
5. **Trigger definition** — what counts as a "sale"? (Asset deal, share deal, majority sale, liquidation event)
6. **Anti-dilution / adjustment clause** — if real equity gets diluted later, what happens to phantom?

## Country-specific note

In many jurisdictions, phantom equity is treated as a wage component for tax purposes → payout at trigger is taxed as income, not capital gains. Often still attractive vs. the complications of real shares with notarization etc.

→ **Consult a tax advisor before implementation.**

# Proximity Principle — Environment Shapes Outcomes

> *"His parents recognized really early on that the environment he was in wasn't built for him. So they reorganized all of their priorities around one goal — getting him into a different environment, one where he actually had a shot."*

## The principle

You become the average of the 5 people you spend the most time with. You become the level of the rooms you move in. You become the standard of what's "normal" around you.

Proximity isn't networking. Proximity is the *physical, geographic, social, digital* position you occupy.

## How to look at it

You're not a victim of your environment — you're a co-designer. Every choice about where you work, who you meet, which calls you take, shapes your output.

Expanding = what one believes is possible grows.

## When you're not in the "right city"

> *"That doesn't mean if you aren't in a specific country or city that you have no chance. Applying this principle can be as small as working in the best hotel lobby in your city, or attending virtual events where you'll be meeting the right kind of people."*

Proximity is now also digital:
- Communities (Slack, Discord, Circle)
- Mastermind groups (online)
- Conferences (in person + replay)
- Mentor / coach relationships (Zoom)
- Co-working in the right rooms

## How to apply it

**Monthly proximity action:**

One conscious proximity expansion per month:
- Attend a new event
- Join a new community and actively participate
- Seek a conversation that lifts you one level above
- Ask a coach / mentor (see `../05-people-and-talent/06-asking-for-help.md`)

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Run the proximity audit.

- [ ] Establish monthly routine: deliberate proximity expansion.

## Warning

Proximity alone changes nothing. Proximity + active participation + consistent value contribution — that's what changes things.

Standing in a room isn't enough. Speaking, contributing, taking responsibility.

# Persistence — "Not giving up is the most heroic thing you can do."

> *"Not giving up is the most heroic thing you can do."* — Aquaman poster (Sam's talisman)

## The principle

Persistence isn't *"keep going when you know you'll win."* That's trust — that's easy. Persistence is *"keep going when you no longer know if you'll win — and do it anyway."*

## What this story shows

Persistent low points:
- Mugged in an alley on the first US day
- First night in country — eventually dumpster-diving for pizza and Pop-Tarts
- First exit money reduced 15x by a clause that wasn't read
- 39 (!) one-on-one interviews to get into Goldman Sachs
- Reverse mortgage on the family home to save a company
- $1M lost to a fraudster
- Dark phases of self-doubt as a CEO with no experience

→ Each could have been an end point. None were.

## How to look at it

Assume: **There will be phases when you no longer believe it'll work.** The question isn't *if*, but *when*.

Preparing for those phases is part of the strategy — not a sign of weakness.

## Preparing for dark phases

### 1. Memory anchor
Something tangible that reminds you why you started. (For Sam: the Aquaman poster.)

### 2. Allies (see `../05-people-and-talent/05-allies-vs-energy-drainers.md`)
At least one person who believes in you when you don't.

### 3. Coach / mentor
Outside perspective in the phase where your inside view is cloudy (see `../05-people-and-talent/06-asking-for-help.md`).

### 4. Buffer (see `02-save-for-rainy-day.md`)
Cash reserves that give you time — time to ride through the phase.

### 5. Story-awareness
Knowing the low is part of the journey. Not the end.

## The lesson from the mugger

> *"He says: you're the worst person I ever mugged. I gather some courage and tell him: I'm lost. I just need to get to school tomorrow. I pull out $100. If I give you this $100, will you give me $50 back?"*

Persistence + situational creativity + asking for help in the darkest second. Result: $45 returned and Pop-Tarts.

→ In every low phase there's a creative answer we miss in panic mode.

## Practice: the "low-phase plan"

Write in advance — before the low arrives:

| Element | Content |
|---|---|
| Who's the ally I call? | __ |
| Which memory helps me? | __ |
| Which coach / mentor is briefed? | __ |
| How many months of runway do I have? | __ |
| What mini-action am I allowed to take today? | __ |
| What am I NOT allowed to decide today? | __ |

In the low: read this doc, follow one action.

## What we never forget

It's not that we never fall. It's that we get back up.

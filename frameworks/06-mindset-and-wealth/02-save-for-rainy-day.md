# Save for a Rainy Day — The Buffett Principle

> *"Do not save what is left after spending. Spend what is left after saving."* — Warren Buffett

## The principle

Order matters. Those who save what's left after spending save almost nothing. Those who spend what's left after saving have reserves.

## How to look at it

Saving isn't a moral act. It's **operational preparation** for:
- Unexpected drops (customer leaves, channel breaks)
- Strategic opportunities (hire, investment, acquisition)
- Courage for pivots (a 7x revenue lift after a late pivot is only possible if cash was preserved)

## The 6-month rule

> *"75% of VC-backed startups never return investors capital. Most people don't lack resources — they lack resourcefulness."*

Goal: **6 months runway**. If revenue stops tomorrow, you can keep going for 6 months.

| Cash reserve | Meaning |
|---|---|
| < 1 month | Crisis. Act now. |
| 1–3 months | Tight. Risk building up. |
| 3–6 months | Stable. Operations OK. |
| 6+ months | Strategic. Can attack. |
| 12+ months | Invulnerable. |

## How to apply it

**Step-by-step implementation:**

1. **Determine current cash buffer** — how many months at zero revenue?
2. **Set target buffer** — at least 6 months.
3. **Treat minimum savings rate as a fixed cost** — every month a defined percentage is set aside *first*, before expenses.
4. **Separate account** — savings need to be out of operational sight.
5. **Quarterly check** — target vs actual.

## When funds are tight

> *"Resourcefulness is a skill — and this is my opportunity to sharpen it."*

If cash is currently low, the more important investment is in resourcefulness — doing more with less.

- Streamline processes
- Critically question expenses
- Consolidate tools
- Automate manual tasks
- Make waste visible

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Determine current cash reserve (in months of runway).
- [ ] Calculate gap to 6-month target.
- [ ] Set monthly savings amount that hits target within 12 months.
- [ ] Set up a separate account (if not already).
- [ ] Auto-transfer at month start (before any spending).
- [ ] Measure quarterly.

# Freeze Lifestyle — Keep Your Monthly Nut Flat

> *"In the last 14 years, our family runs on the same monthly nut. Our net worth is probably 50 times in the last 14 years, and we still run on the same monthly nut. It gives us a lot of optionality."* — Sam Shvatsa

## The principle

If your income rises, your lifestyle rises automatically — unless you actively prevent it. That's a defaults trap.

Lifestyle freeze: your monthly fixed need stays **stable**, regardless of income. Everything above the freeze is optionality — reinvestment, reserve, risk-adjustment, choice.

## Why this is critical

> *"$500K a year, after all expenses I made $150K. To hire a really good person, I need to pay $200K. I don't have it. If I hire that person, I'd go to zero."*

→ Founders can't scale because they themselves became too expensive.

> *"When you grow to $1M, you make $300K, and you're used to a $300K lifestyle, and you still can't hire the $250K person."*

Lifestyle inflation = scaling brake.

## How to look at it

Your monthly nut is not "what we can afford." It's **what we deliberately chose** as the floor that enables the entire game.

With a low floor:
- You can make the decisive hire
- You survive crises
- You can take risks others can't
- You have choice (optionality)

## How to apply it

**Lifestyle audit (once a year):**

| Category | Current per month | Conscious? Necessary? |
|---|---|---|
| Housing | __ | __ |
| Vehicle / mobility | __ | __ |
| Groceries | __ | __ |
| Leisure / restaurants | __ | __ |
| Subscriptions | __ | __ |
| Clothing / consumption | __ | __ |
| Other fixed costs | __ | __ |

**Per row:** *"If I halved this expense, would my quality of life noticeably change?"*

Often: no. → Candidate for freeze level.

## Practical rules

1. **Define a monthly nut.** Concrete number, all fixed costs + standard living.
2. **Don't raise it without a deliberate decision.** Raise = memo (see `../07-communication-and-marketing/01-memo-culture-wafam.md`).
3. **Live below it, not above.** Even when income rises.
4. **Reinvest / save the difference** — don't spend it.

## Concrete actions

- [ ] Track current monthly expenses (3 months, precisely).
- [ ] Define monthly nut — a deliberate number.
- [ ] Identify the gap to income.
- [ ] Set allocation: % reinvest, % reserve, % quality-of-life investment.
- [ ] On income rise: do NOT auto-raise lifestyle.

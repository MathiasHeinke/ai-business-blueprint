# Cheatsheet — One Page, Pinned to the Wall

> Print this. Tape it next to your screen. Use it daily.

---

## The 3 sentences that change everything

1. **"What's the one thing — if I took it away — would not be valuable for you?"**
   → reveals your real value prop.

2. **"Assume I've already accomplished this goal. Does everything else still work?"**
   → finds bottlenecks before they find you.

3. **"No memo, no meeting."**
   → forces clarity, eliminates theater.

---

## The 4-step decision

1. **Context** — what we know
2. **Issue** — the actual problem
3. **Risk** — worst case, can we live with it?
4. **Map** — who, what, when

---

## The TSS diagnostic

| Area | Job | Bottleneck if... |
|---|---|---|
| **Traffic** | Fill the funnel | Volume / quality low |
| **Systems** | Convert opportunities | Drop-off, manual work |
| **Skills** | Deliver and get paid | Founder-dependent |

→ Fix in this order: **Systems before Traffic**. Always.

---

## The 1-1-1

- **1** Traffic source
- **1** Conversion mechanism
- **1** Delivery channel

→ Dial this first. Then add a second source. Then a third.

---

## The 3 questions for any problem

1. What could I **do**?
2. What could I **read**?
3. **Whom** could I ask?

→ Question 3 is the underused one. Every time.

---

## The 4-step hire

1. Write the pain (or aspiration) raw and ugly.
2. Feed it to AI for a JD draft.
3. Add a self-selection question.
4. Recruit actively. Don't post and pray.

---

## The 6-month rule

Cash reserve = months of survival at zero revenue.

| Reserve | Status |
|---|---|
| < 1 month | Crisis |
| 3–6 months | Stable |
| 6+ months | Strategic |

Goal: **always 6 months minimum**.

---

## The lifestyle freeze

> Income up, lifestyle flat.

Differential = options, not lifestyle. The longer you keep the floor flat, the more leverage you have.

---

## The 12 beliefs (memorize these)

1. Complexity kills scale.
2. Subtraction beats addition.
3. Capability ≠ justification.
4. Bottlenecks are singular.
5. Plumbing before water.
6. Customers know the answer — ask them.
7. No memo, no decision.
8. Lifestyle drift is the silent founder killer.
9. Wealth is a "who" strategy.
10. Build to be sellable.
11. Asking is not weakness.
12. Persistence is heroic.

---

## When in doubt

> *"What would the laziest possible version of this look like?"*

If you can answer that and still get the result — that's the version.

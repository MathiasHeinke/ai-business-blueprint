# Attribution & Sources

## Origin

The AI Business Blueprint is a **distillation, structuring, and translation** of the operating principles shared by **Sam Shvatsa** in his interview on the *Anatomy of a Dream* podcast hosted by **Tiffany**.

Sam Shvatsa is the CEO of [acquisition.com](https://acquisition.com), an investment partnership with Alex and Leila Hormozi. His operator background includes:
- Investment Banking at Goldman Sachs
- CEO of Telos Properties (scaled from $300M to $3.4B in 5 years)
- Multiple successful exits

The interview content covers his frameworks for diagnosis, simplicity, customer discovery, talent acquisition, decision-making, and personal-finance discipline.

## What this repository is — and is not

**This repo IS:**
- An open-source educational resource
- A structured re-presentation of widely-shared business principles
- A workable template for entrepreneurs who want to apply these ideas with the help of AI assistants
- Original synthesis and structuring, including:
  - The 9-module organization
  - The "AI co-pilot" framing and prompt library
  - The implementation plans (7-day, 30-60-90, quarterly cadence)
  - The diagnostic checklist
  - The repository format itself

**This repo is NOT:**
- A transcript of the interview
- An official product of acquisition.com, Sam Shvatsa, Alex Hormozi, Leila Hormozi, *Anatomy of a Dream*, or Tiffany
- An endorsement by any of the above (none has been requested or granted)
- A replacement for direct study of these operators' work

If you find this useful, **the highest-leverage thing you can do is also study the source**: watch the original interviews, listen to the *Build* podcast (Hormozi), and read the books and posts these operators publish directly.

## Tone and voice

The framework files in this repo use direct, declarative voice. That voice is a synthesis — not a transcription. Quotes that appear in `> blockquotes` are presented as paraphrases of public statements unless explicitly marked as direct quotation. Nothing in this repo should be cited as a verbatim source.

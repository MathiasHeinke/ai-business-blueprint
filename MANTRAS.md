# MANTRAS — Daily Anchors

> 12 sentences for 12 days, then start over. Read one each morning. Don't read all 12 at once — that's noise.

---

## Day 1
> *To do great things, we must do fewer things.*

If today's plan has more than 3 priorities, two are wrong.

---

## Day 2
> *Build the plumbing before sending water through the pipes.*

Don't promote what isn't ready to deliver. Fix the system first.

---

## Day 3
> *Capability is not justification.*

You can do many things. That doesn't mean you should. Cut today.

---

## Day 4
> *No memo, no decision.*

Anything worth deciding is worth writing down. Write something today.

---

## Day 5
> *Bottlenecks are singular.*

There is one. Find it. Fix it. Don't waste energy on the others.

---

## Day 6
> *Customers know the answer. Stop guessing.*

Two questions. Ten customers. The rest is noise.

---

## Day 7
> *Lifestyle drift is the silent killer.*

The income went up. Don't let the lifestyle follow.

---

## Day 8
> *Wealth is a who-strategy, not a what-strategy.*

Pick 10 people for 10 years. Invest. Compound.

---

## Day 9
> *Build to be sellable, even if you never sell.*

That's the standard. The byproduct is a better business.

---

## Day 10
> *Asking is not weakness — it's compounding.*

What could I do? What could I read? Whom could I ask? Always question 3.

---

## Day 11
> *Persistence is heroic, especially when invisible.*

The result you want is on the other side of the part you don't want to do.

---

## Day 12
> *Subtraction beats addition.*

Cut something today. Measure for 30 days. See what actually breaks.

---

## How to use this

- One mantra per day. Read on your phone, in your journal, on a sticky note.
- After 12 days, start at Day 1 again. Notice which sentence lands differently this round.
- After 4 cycles (~6 weeks), you've internalized them.

These aren't motivation. They're calibration.
